About Me

Hi, I’m Lee James Friedman! I also go by LJ. I’m currently pursuing a degree in Computer Science with minors in Math, Music, and Psychology at Whitman College. Originally from the Bay Area in California, I’ve always been fascinated by the intersection of technology, social justice, and creative expression.

As part of my academic pursuits, I’ve written several papers on various projects, which are available in the "Design Posts" tab in the header. Data Sources and Project Ideas

  1. Stock Market and U.S. Economy

    Description: This dataset includes DJIA data from the last hundred years. All data is inflation-adjusted.

    Project Idea: Explore relationships between U.S. presidential elections and stocks — specifically observing short-term impacts (surrounding election day) and long-term impacts (general trend during the 4 years of presidency).

  2. U.S. Census Data

    Description: Contains census data such as population ages, state of residence, racial demographics, etc.

    Project Idea: Investigate the relationship between a state's shifting demographics over time and their political affiliations, using results of local and state elections.

  3. Movies and the Bechdel Test

    Description: A collection of data on movies, featuring the year of release, whether they pass the Bechdel test, their budget, and their gross earnings.

    Project Idea: Determine whether or not passing the Bechdel test is correlated with higher gross earnings for movies (and see if this changes depending on what decade you look at).

  4. Airline Crashes and Fatalities

    Description: This data include information on airlines, how many seats flown per week, and total number of incidents pre-2000 and post-2000.

    Project Idea: Explore relationships between airline size, crash rate, and whether or not crash rates remain consistent before and after the year 2000.

  5. College Majors and Outcomes

    Description: Provides data on college majors and graduates, as well as jobs and income levels post-graduation.

    Project Idea: Study the differences between post-graduate incomes and gender, as well as examine any correlations between overall proportions of women in certain majors and average post-graduate income.
